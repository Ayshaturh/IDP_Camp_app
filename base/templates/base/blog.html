Struggles of Chinedu in Lagos

Chinedu is a 12-year-old boy living in Lagos. His family was forced to leave their home due to violent conflicts in their village. Now, in the bustling city, Chinedu struggles to find enough food and clean water. Despite these challenges, he remains hopeful and attends a makeshift school where he dreams of becoming a doctor one day.

Amina's Journey in Kano

Amina, an 11-year-old girl from Kano, lost her parents to a tragic accident. Now living with her grandmother, Amina helps in selling vegetables in the market to support their livelihood. Despite the hardships, she continues her education and aspires to become a teacher to help other children in her community.

Emeka's Dream in Abuja

Emeka, a 14-year-old boy from Abuja, faced displacement when his village was attacked. Now residing in an IDP camp, he uses his free time to learn carpentry skills from volunteers. Emeka dreams of opening his own workshop one day to provide for his family and give back to his community.

Zainab's Hope in Jos

Zainab, a 13-year-old girl from Jos, witnessed her home being destroyed during a conflict. Living in an overcrowded IDP camp, she finds solace in drawing and painting, expressing her feelings and hopes through art. Zainab hopes to become an artist and inspire others with her story of resilience.

Tunde's Resilience in Maiduguri

Tunde, a 10-year-old boy from Maiduguri, was separated from his family during an attack. Now living with distant relatives in an IDP camp, he helps out by fetching water and doing odd jobs. Tunde's love for football keeps his spirit alive, and he dreams of becoming a professional player to bring joy to his community.
